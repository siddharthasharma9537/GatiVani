## What is GatiVani?

GatiVani transforms Telugu newspaper articles into podcast-style audio so you can stay informed during your commute, exercise, or any time your hands are busy. The app pulls fresh articles from five major Telugu newspapers and uses AI to read them aloud naturally in Telugu, English, or Hindi.

**Supported newspapers:**
- Andhra Jyothi
- Namasthe Telangana
- Sakshi
- Andhra Prabha
- Prajasakti

## Getting Started

### Installing the app

**Android**: Search "GatiVani" on Google Play and tap Install.

**iOS**: Search "GatiVani" on the App Store and tap Get.

**Web**: Visit [gativani.app](https://gativani.app) — no installation needed.

### First launch

When you open GatiVani for the first time:

1. The app asks for permission to send you notifications. Tap **Allow** to receive breaking-news alerts. You can change this later in Settings.
2. The home screen loads with today's articles from all five newspapers.
3. Tap any article card to start listening immediately.

No account or sign-in is required to use GatiVani.

## Home Screen

The home screen is your news feed. At a glance you can see:

- **Article cards** — headline, newspaper name, publication time, and featured image.
- **Source filter bar** — a horizontal strip of newspaper chips at the top. Tap a chip to show only articles from that paper; tap it again to deselect.
- **Search bar** — tap the magnifying glass to search across all loaded articles.
- **Bottom navigation** — Home, Bookmarks, Settings.

### Article card indicators

| Badge | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Checkmark on image | You have already listened to this article |
| Filled bookmark icon | Article is saved to your bookmarks |
| Newspaper name chip | Which paper the article is from |

Articles are sorted newest first. Pull down on the feed to refresh.

## Listening to an Article

Tap any article card to open the full player. GatiVani will:

1. Summarize the article text using AI (takes 2–5 seconds on a first listen).
2. Convert the summary to natural-sounding speech.
3. Begin playback automatically.

If you have a slow connection, a loading spinner appears while the audio is being prepared. Once generated, the audio is cached so replaying the same article is instant.

## Audio Player Controls

The player screen shows the article headline and newspaper name at the top, followed by the full player controls.

### Playback controls

| Button | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Play / Pause | Start or pause the audio |
| Skip forward 10 s | Jump ahead 10 seconds |
| Rewind 10 s | Go back 10 seconds |
| Next article | Load the next article in the feed |
| Previous article | Go back to the previous article |

### Progress bar

Drag the slider to jump to any point in the audio. The current time and total duration are shown on either side.

### Playback speed

Tap the **speed button** (shows "1.0×" by default) to open a speed picker. Available speeds: 0.5×, 0.75×, 1×, 1.25×, 1.5×, 2×. Your preferred speed is saved between sessions.

### Bookmark and share

- Tap the **bookmark icon** in the top-right corner of the player to save the article.
- Tap the **share icon** to share the article link via any app on your device.

### Background playback

Audio continues playing when you lock your screen or switch to another app. Use your device's lock screen controls or notification panel to play, pause, or skip.

## Searching for News

Tap the search icon on the home screen to open the search bar.

- Type at least 2 characters to start searching.
- Results update as you type (with a short delay to avoid unnecessary requests).
- Tap the **X** button to clear the search and return to the full feed.
- Tap the **filter icon** next to the search bar to open the source filter panel.

### Recent searches

The search bar remembers your last 5 searches. Tap any recent search to run it again. Tap **Clear** to wipe the history.

---

## Filtering by Source

You can filter the feed to show only articles from specific newspapers.

**Quick filter (home screen)**: Tap a newspaper chip in the horizontal bar below the search bar. Multiple chips can be selected at the same time.

**Full filter panel**: Tap the filter icon in the search bar to open the full source filter. From here you can:
- Select or deselect individual newspapers.
- Tap the menu (⋮) and choose **Select All** or **Deselect All**.
- See a count of how many sources are currently active.

---

## Bookmarks

Bookmarked articles are saved to the **Bookmarks** tab (second icon in the bottom navigation).

- Tap the bookmark icon on any article card or in the player to toggle the bookmark.
- Bookmarks are stored on your device and persist between app restarts.
- Swipe left on a bookmark to remove it.
- Bookmarked articles can be listened to even if they have been removed from the live feed.

## Settings

Open **Settings** from the bottom navigation (gear icon).

| Setting | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Language | Choose your preferred playback language: Telugu, English, or Hindi |
| Default playback speed | Set the speed that loads for every new article |
| Notifications | Toggle push notifications on or off |
| Clear cache | Free up storage by removing cached audio files |
| App version | Displays the current version number |

## Offline Listening

Articles you have already listened to are cached on your device. To listen offline:

1. Listen to articles while connected to the internet — they are cached automatically.
2. When you go offline, cached articles are still available and play normally.
3. New articles require an internet connection to load and generate audio.

The cache holds up to 100 items. Older cached articles are removed automatically when the cache is full. You can also clear the cache manually in Settings.

## Notifications

GatiVani can notify you when important news arrives. To manage notifications:

**On Android**: Settings > Apps > GatiVani > Notifications.

**On iOS**: Settings > GatiVani > Notifications.

**In the app**: Settings > Notifications toggle.

## Languages Supported

GatiVani supports three languages for audio playback:

| Code | Language | Script |
|------|----------|--------|
| `te` | Telugu | Telugu (Default) |
| `en` | English | Latin |
| `hi` | Hindi | Devanagari |

Switch your preferred language in Settings. The AI will summarize and read articles in your chosen language regardless of the original article language.

Telugu text is rendered using the **Mallanna** and **NotoSerifTelugu** fonts, which are bundled with the app for correct glyph display even without internet access.
